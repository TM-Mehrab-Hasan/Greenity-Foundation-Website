Dhaka, a metropolis defined by its rapid vertical growth, has reached a critical tipping point in its ecological health. The city's once-vibrant natural canopy has been systematically displaced by concrete, leading to a dangerous intensification of the **Urban Heat Island Effect**.

### The Climate Challenge
Our recent thermal mapping indicated that core urban sectors are now up to 4°C warmer than the surrounding rural peripheries. This escalation in temperature, combined with deteriorating air quality, represents a direct threat to public health and urban livability.

### A Scientific Approach to Reforestation
The **Green Canopy Project** serves as a strategic counter-offensive. Rather than random planting, our team utilized ecological mapping to identify priority "Heat Zones." Our intervention focused on:
- **Biodiversity Corridors:** Reintroducing indigenous species like Neem, Bakul, and Jam to restore natural cooling cycles.
- **Pedestrian Shading:** Transforming sun-scorched residential streets into walkable green transit routes.
- **Educational Hubs:** Establishing micro-forests within school grounds to provide physical shading and environmental awareness for the next generation.

### Measurable Progress
Through the dedicated efforts of **850 volunteers**, we have successfully integrated **5,000 native saplings** into the urban fabric. These are not merely trees; they are living infrastructure designed to sequester carbon and mitigate thermal runoff.

> "Urban resilience is not built with concrete alone. It is grown. Every sapling planted today is a deliberate act of defiance against a warming climate." — Ratul Ahmed, Environmental Strategist.
