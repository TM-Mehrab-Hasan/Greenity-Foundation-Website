The Buriganga River, historically the economic and cultural heart of Dhaka, has long buckled under the weight of systemic neglect and industrial runoff. Our latest intervention—the **Buriganga Restoration Initiative**—was engineered to address the river's ecological degradation through high-impact physical extraction and community policy advocacy.

### The Crisis of Inertia
Decades of unregulated plastic disposal had created a dense, non-biodegradable sediment along the riverbanks. This buildup was not merely an aesthetic failure but a profound ecological crisis, disrupting local aquatic cycles and endangering the health of riparian communities.

### Tactical Methodology
Our operations transitioned from simple volunteer sweeps to a more robust, industrialized approach:
- **Mechanical Debris Extraction:** Utilizing specialized equipment to retrieve submerged industrial waste that hand-tools could not reach.
- **Micro-Hub Logistics:** Establishing localized collection points to minimize the carbon footprint of waste transportation.
- **Policy Mobilization:** Installing 53 high-capacity waste containment units and securing commitments from 100+ local vendors to phase out single-use plastics.

### Verified Impact Data
Over an intensive 21-day period, our teams successfully diverted **5,000 kilograms of synthetic waste** from the river ecosystem. This material has been successfully integrated into our circular economy pipeline, where it is being processed into durable construction materials.

> "The restoration of the Buriganga is a marathon, not a sprint. We are not just clearing water; we are reclaiming the soul of our city." — Lead Technical Coordinator.
